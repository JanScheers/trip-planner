# China Trip Tips

Practical advice for our 21-day China itinerary (October 2026).

## Packing

- **Power adapters**: China uses Type A, C, or I plugs. Bring a universal adapter; 220V.
- **VPN**: Essential for Google, WhatsApp, Gmail. Install before you arrive (e.g. ExpressVPN, Astrill).
- **WeChat Pay**: Set up before the trip if possible; many places are cashless or prefer mobile payment.

## Visa & Documents

- Apply for your Chinese visa well in advance (typically 30–90 days before travel).
- Keep photocopies of your passport and visa; store separately from originals.
- Register with your embassy if required.

## Health

- **Water**: Drink bottled or boiled water only. Avoid ice in drinks unless from trusted sources.
- **Food**: Street food is amazing but start gently; stick to busy stalls with high turnover.
- **Altitude**: Emei Shan and some areas reach 3000m+. Take it slow if you're sensitive.

## Cultural Tips

- **Bargaining**: Expected at markets and some shops. Start around 30–50% of the asking price.
- **Temples**: Dress modestly; remove shoes where indicated; don't point at Buddha statues.
- **Guanxi**: A little politeness and patience go a long way.

## Practical

- **SIM / eSIM**: Get a local SIM or eSIM for data; very affordable and useful for maps/translation.
- **Apps**: WeChat (messaging, payments), Google Translate (download Chinese offline), Maps.me or Amap for navigation.
- **Cash**: Carry some RMB; smaller towns and rural areas may not accept cards or WeChat.
